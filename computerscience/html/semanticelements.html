Understanding Semantic HTML Elements

Semantic HTML elements give meaning to the web page structure. These elements help both browsers and developers better understand the content of a webpage.

  • <header> - Represents the introductory content of a document or section (like a logo, navigation menu, etc.).
  • <footer> - Represents the footer content (like copyright info, contact details, etc.).
  • <article> - Defines an independent piece of content that can stand alone, like a blog post or news article.
  • <section> - Represents a thematic grouping of content, typically with its own heading.
  • <nav> - Defines a navigation section (links to other pages or sections).
  • <aside> - Represents content that's tangentially related to the content around it (like a sidebar or advertisement).
